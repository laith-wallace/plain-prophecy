The First Beast
Babylon
605 – 539 BC
Winged Lion
The Prophecy

"The first was like a lion, and had eagle's wings: I beheld till the wings thereof were plucked, and it was made stand upon the feet as a man, and a man's heart was given to it." The lion — king of the beasts — combined with the eagle, king of birds. Total supremacy. Then a startling change: wings torn off, made to stand like a man, given a human heart. (Daniel 7:4)

History Confirms

Babylon was the most magnificent empire the ancient world had seen. The lion was its national symbol — archaeological evidence shows lions carved into the famous Ishtar Gate. Nebuchadnezzar conquered with eagle-like speed; the prophet Habakkuk wrote that the Babylonians flew like eagles to devour (Habakkuk 1:8). Then the wings were plucked: after Nebuchadnezzar's seven years of madness and his restoration with a humbled heart (Daniel 4), the empire's ferocity ebbed. Under Belshazzar, it stood like a proud man — and fell in a single night.

✦ Christ at the Centre

God brought Daniel to Babylon as a captive teenager and gave him an interpreter's gift no Babylonian wise man possessed. Even at the height of the lion empire, God was preserving — through Daniel — the understanding of prophecy that would culminate in Christ. The same God who humbled Nebuchadnezzar was already writing the story that ends at Calvary.

The Second Beast
Medo-Persia
539 – 331 BC
Bear
The Prophecy

"And behold another beast, a second, like to a bear, and it raised up itself on one side, and it had three ribs in the mouth of it between the teeth of it: and they said thus unto it, Arise, devour much flesh." Three details: a bear (massive, relentless), raised on one side (asymmetric), and three ribs held in its teeth. (Daniel 7:5)

History Confirms

Medo-Persia was the largest empire the world had seen at that time, amassing armies of over a million men — slow and lumbering like a bear, but ferocious. "Raised on one side" perfectly captures its dual nature: the Persians rose to dominate the Medes (Cyrus overthrew the Median Confederation in the 6th century BC). The three ribs match the three major kingdoms it consumed: Lydia fell in 546 BC, Babylon in 539 BC, and Egypt in 525 BC. Three nations between its teeth, exactly as prophesied.

✦ Christ at the Centre

Cyrus — named by Isaiah 150 years before he was born (Isaiah 44:28) — issued the decree to restore Jerusalem. That decree in 457 BC launched the prophetic 70-week countdown that ended precisely at Jesus' baptism in AD 27. A pagan bear-empire, unknowingly obeying God, set the clock for the Messiah's arrival. The entire sequence was scripted centuries in advance.

The Third Beast
Greece
331 – 168 BC
Leopard
The Prophecy

"After this I beheld, and lo another, like a leopard, which had upon the back of it four wings of a fowl; the beast had also four heads; and dominion was given to it." Four wings — double the speed of Babylon's two. Four heads — four distinct rulers within one kingdom. (Daniel 7:6)

History Confirms

Alexander the Great conquered the entire Persian empire in thirteen years, dying at just 33. The four wings capture what historians still marvel at: a campaign of unprecedented speed. The leopard is fast; four wings of a bird make it almost supernatural in velocity. When Alexander died in 323 BC with no heir, his generals divided his empire into exactly four parts — Lysimachus took the north, Cassander the west, Ptolemy the south (Egypt), and Seleucus the east. Four heads. Written by Daniel centuries before Greece was even a unified nation.

✦ Christ at the Centre

Greek conquest spread Koine Greek across the entire known world — the language the New Testament would be written in. Under the Greek empire, the Hebrew scriptures were translated into Greek (the Septuagint), making them accessible to the whole Mediterranean world. God used an empire built on conquest to build the communication infrastructure for the Gospel. By the time Jesus was born, the whole world could read his story.

The Fourth Beast
Rome
168 BC – 476 AD
Terrible Beast
The Prophecy

"After this I saw in the night visions, and behold a fourth beast, dreadful and terrible, and strong exceedingly; and it had great iron teeth: it devoured and brake in pieces, and stamped the residue with the feet of it: and it was diverse from all the beasts that were before it; and it had ten horns." No known animal could describe it — Rome was in a category of its own. (Daniel 7:7)

History Confirms

Rome at its height controlled nearly five million square kilometres, devouring nation after nation. Daniel's iron-teeth description mirrors Daniel 2's iron legs exactly: the same empire confirmed twice. Unlike its predecessors, Rome didn't conquer for glory alone — it systematically destroyed and absorbed, stamping residue underfoot. When Rome eventually collapsed, it fragmented into ten Germanic kingdoms (Visigoths, Franks, Anglo-Saxons, Lombards, Suevi, Allemani, Burgundians, Heruli, Vandals, Ostrogoths) — exactly ten horns, which became the major nations of Western Europe. Hippolytus, a 2nd-century Roman theologian, interpreted these ten horns as the kingdoms arising from Rome, more than a century before it happened.

✦ Christ at the Centre

Jesus was born under Roman rule — "a decree from Caesar Augustus" (Luke 2:1). He was crucified by Rome. His resurrection happened under Roman watch. And then the terrible beast became the unwitting infrastructure for the Gospel: Roman roads connected the empire; Paul's Roman citizenship protected his ministry; Roman legal courts gave him a platform all the way to Caesar. God used the most brutal empire in history to carry the message of the most compassionate person in history.

The Little Horn
A Power Unlike Any Before
476 AD – Present
Little Horn
The Prophecy

"And another shall rise after them; he shall be different from the first ones, and shall subdue three kings. He shall speak pompous words against the Most High, shall persecute the saints of the Most High, and shall intend to change times and law. Then the saints shall be given into his hand for a time and times and half a time." A horn — not a beast — rising among Rome's ten successor kingdoms, uprooting three of them, claiming religious and civil authority, persecuting the faithful for 3.5 prophetic times. (Daniel 7:24–25)

History Confirms

One power in history satisfies every criterion Daniel gives. It rose from within the Roman system (not alongside it). It uprooted three kingdoms: the Heruli (AD 493), the Vandals (AD 534), and the Ostrogoths (AD 538). It claimed a human head who spoke for God. It persecuted the saints during centuries of inquisition. It changed times and law — claiming authority to alter the Sabbath. The prophetic 3.5 times, using the day-year principle established in Numbers 14:34 and Ezekiel 4:6, equals 1,260 years. From 538 AD to 1798 AD is exactly 1,260 years — when Napoleon's general arrested the Pope, ending the political supremacy. The precision is extraordinary.

✦ Christ at the Centre

Even through 1,260 years of persecution, God's Word could not be silenced. The Waldensians hid the scriptures in the mountain valleys of northern Italy. Wycliffe translated them into English. Hus died for preaching them. Luther nailed his theses and the Reformation broke open. The little horn could "make war with the saints" — but Daniel's vision ends with the judgement, not the persecution. The Ancient of Days sits. And the horn's dominion is taken away.

The Heavenly Judgment
Ancient of Days & Son of Man
Daniel 7:9–14 · The Climax of History
Eternal Kingdom
The Prophecy

"I watched till thrones were put in place, and the Ancient of Days was seated; His garment was white as snow, and the hair of His head was like pure wool. His throne was a fiery flame... A river of fire was issuing and coming out from before Him... The court was seated, and the books were opened." And then: "One like the Son of Man, coming with the clouds of heaven... He was given dominion and glory and a kingdom, that all peoples, nations, and languages should serve Him. His dominion is an everlasting dominion, which shall not pass away." (Daniel 7:9–14)

What It Means

Every beast, every empire, every horn — all brought before a heavenly court of justice. The books are opened. The little horn's dominion is stripped away. And then the defining moment: a figure like a Son of Man approaches the Ancient of Days and receives a kingdom that will never end. Unlike every earthly empire — which rose by conquest and fell by conquest — this kingdom is given. No armies. No strategy. A coronation in heaven that ends with a new earth.

✦ Christ at the Centre

"Son of Man" was Jesus' favourite title for himself — used more than eighty times in the Gospels. When the High Priest demanded "Are you the Christ?" Jesus answered directly: "You will see the Son of Man seated at the right hand of Power, and coming with the clouds of heaven." (Mark 14:62) He was quoting Daniel 7:13. He wasn't being evasive — he was announcing that he was the one Daniel saw approaching the Ancient of Days. The judgment, the kingdom, the dominion — all of it, given to Christ. Every beast that rose falls silent. And he reigns.
